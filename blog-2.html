Sydney's $8B Metro West Link Aims for Fast Commutes to CBD and Airport

Badgery Creek, NSW
  1. Home
  2. High Speed Trains
  • Date: 15 Sep. 2017

The NSW and federal governments are considering a groundbreaking proposal for a high-speed metro rail line, offering fast and convenient travel options for commuters in Western Sydney. The project, dubbed the Metro West Link, aims to provide a direct and rapid transport link between the CBD and Parramatta, while also linking major economic zones throughout the city.

The ambitious $8 billion plan, largely funded by a private consortium, promises to take passengers from Central Station to Badgerys Creek Airport in just 34 minutes. Additionally, the journey from the city to Parramatta would take a mere 18 minutes. The proposed rail link will connect several key precincts, including Barangaroo, the Darling Harbour centre, the Bays precinct, Strathfield, Olympic Park, Camellia, Parramatta, and Westmead.

The second stage of the project will extend to the western Sydney employment precinct, Badgerys Creek, which has been dubbed "Airport City", and the southwest growth centre. This expansion will enable commuters to travel between Parramatta and the new airport in just 16 minutes - an impressive feat that is comparable to the travel time between the CBD and Sydney Airport at Mascot. The Metro West Link is an exciting and innovative development that promises to revolutionize public transport in Western Sydney, providing a fast and efficient means of transportation for residents, tourists, and businesspeople alike.
